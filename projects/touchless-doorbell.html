Arduino-Based Touchless Doorbell

Overview: This project showcases a touchless doorbell system built using Arduino UNO. Designed by a team of three, it leverages a PIR sensor to detect motion and activate a buzzer, alerting the residents of a visitor without the need for contact. It's a compact and efficient application of automation in daily life.

Key Components:

  • Arduino UNO
  • PIR Motion Sensor
  • Buzzer
  • Resistors
  • Bulb (optional for indicators)
  • Jumper Wires
  • Breadboard

Working Mechanism:

When someone comes close to the door, the PIR sensor detects motion and sends a signal to the Arduino. The Arduino then triggers the buzzer, which acts as a doorbell. The system is clean, contactless, and works without any need for pressing a button.

Pros:

  • Completely contactless and safe
  • Cost-effective and easy to implement
  • Beginner-friendly with scope for smart home upgrades

Cons:

  • May trigger due to pets or minor movement
  • Requires stable indoor conditions
  • Limited detection range by PIR sensor
